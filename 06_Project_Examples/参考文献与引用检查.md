> _Spartina alterniflora_ (smooth cordgrass) is a highly invasive clonal plant in global coastal salt marshes **(Strong & Ayres, 2013)**. Due to its extensive coastal range, previous evolutionary studies on _S. alterniflora_ have largely focused on macrogeographic adaptive clines spanning continental latitudinal gradients **(Qiao et al., 2019)**. > 
> During local expansion, sequentially established populations form chronological patches over just a few years. Acclimating to such gradients requires hierarchical biological partitioning: from baseline genetic sorting during founder events to shifts in gene expression, metabolic regulation, and ultimate structural resource allocation. Because these biological layers are **often examined independently in wild populations (Scossa et al., 2021)**, the causal pathways translating local establishment history into multi-omics dynamics and whole-plant phenotypes remain entangled.
