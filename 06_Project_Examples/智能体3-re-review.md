# Editorial Decision Package

## Part 1: Editorial Decision Letter

Dear Author(s),

Thank you for submitting your manuscript titled **“Microgeographic differentiation and coordinated leaf-level molecular shifts during invasion of _Spartina alterniflora_”** for re-evaluation. The manuscript has been assessed through a structured narrow re-review process based on the prior review architecture and the current multi-perspective reassessment.

### Decision: **Minor Revision**

### Final Quality Score

- **Normalized Score**: **68/100**
- **Decision Band**: **65–79 = Minor Revision**
- **Mode**: **re-review**
- **Stage**: **Stage 3' — Narrow re-review**

### Consensus Analysis

#### Points of Agreement

- **[CONSENSUS-4]** The revised manuscript is **materially improved** relative to the earlier version, especially in reducing overclaiming and aligning claims more closely with the observational chronosequence design.
- **[CONSENSUS-4]** The strongest defensible contribution is now a **cautious observational/integrative ecology story**: nearby patches differ across phenotype, SNP structure, transcriptome, and metabolome, while direct omics-to-trait links remain weak or uncertain.
- **[CONSENSUS-4]** The matched integrative model remains constrained by a **small and imbalanced subset (n = 20)** and therefore should be interpreted cautiously.
- **[CONSENSUS-4]** **PC1-only dimensional reduction** remains a central residual limitation, especially given the low variance explained for metabolomics.
- **[CONSENSUS-3]** The manuscript is now potentially publishable for a **fit-for-scope invasion ecology / observational integrative ecology venue**, provided final framing and transparency corrections are made.
- **[CONSENSUS-3]** Remaining issues are now primarily about **framing, methodological clarification, and boundary-setting**, rather than fundamental rejection-level flaws.

#### Points of Disagreement

- **[Journal-level acceptability of residual methodological fragility]**:
    
    - **Reviewer positions**:
        - **EIC**: close to acceptable, with Major Revision leaning Minor Revision if positioned for an appropriate journal.
        - **R1**: still Major Revision because the integrative model remains statistically fragile and insufficiently justified.
        - **R2**: broadly positive; Minor-to-Major boundary, with ecological framing now largely acceptable.
    - **Editorial resolution**: **Minor Revision**
    - **Rationale**: This is a **Stage 3' re-review**, so the editorial question is not whether all limitations disappeared, but whether the remaining limitations are now sufficiently disclosed and proportionate to the revised claims. On that question, the answer is **mostly yes**. The remaining weaknesses are serious enough to require targeted revision, but they are no longer of the same order as the prior overclaiming problem.
- **[Need for additional sensitivity analysis versus strengthened caveating alone]**:
    
    - **Reviewer positions**:
        - **R1** and **DA** favor at least some further robustness clarification and would prefer sensitivity reporting if available.
        - **EIC** suggests that sharper framing and explicit methodological caution may suffice for a fit-for-scope venue.
        - **R2** is comfortable if process claims remain restrained.
    - **Editorial resolution**: **Sensitivity analysis is desirable but not mandatory if unavailable; however, the manuscript must explicitly state the exploratory status and analytical consequences of the modeling choices.**
    - **Rationale**: The record does not support imposing a wholly new methodological agenda at re-review. But the paper still needs a clearer statement of what the model can and cannot establish, and if any robustness checks were attempted, they should be briefly reported.

#### DA-Critical Issues

- **[Single-axis compression may overstate integrative coordination]**:
    
    - **DA argument**: The apparent coordination across levels may depend too heavily on PC1-only summaries, especially where metabolomic PC1 explains only 10.0% of variance.
    - **Corroboration from other reviewers**: Strongly corroborated by **R1**, partially by **EIC**.
    - **Editorial assessment**: **Validated concern, but mitigated by the revised manuscript’s more cautious framing.**
    - **Required author response**: Explicitly justify PC1-only use as a reduced exploratory summary; acknowledge that weak omics–phenotype links may reflect summary-axis choice as well as biology.
- **[Narrative centrality exceeds representativeness of the matched subset]**:
    
    - **DA argument**: The manuscript’s integrative story rests on a filtered, imbalanced 20-sample subset that may not represent the broader system.
    - **Corroboration from other reviewers**: Supported by **EIC**, **R1**, and indirectly by **R2**.
    - **Editorial assessment**: **Validated concern.** This does not invalidate the paper, but it limits how centrally the integrative model should be positioned.
    - **Required author response**: Reduce any residual rhetorical centrality of the hierarchical model and reiterate that it is based on a restricted matched subset after metabolomics batch-related exclusion.

### Decision Rationale

This re-review indicates a manuscript that has **substantially improved** in the areas that previously posed the greatest editorial risk. Most importantly, the revised version no longer presents the study as strong evidence for rapid evolution, resolved mechanism, or system-wide decoupling. The title, abstract, and discussion are now much better aligned with what the data can support: **microgeographic differentiation across biological levels in an observational invasion landscape, with only limited evidence for direct leaf-omics-to-trait prediction**.

That improvement matters. In a re-review context, editorial synthesis must respect closure discipline: the standard is not whether the paper has become methodologically ideal, but whether the authors have brought the manuscript into a zone where the claims are proportionate to the evidence. In this case, the answer is **yes, but not fully without one more round of targeted clarification**.

The remaining concerns are real and traceable across the review set: the integrative hierarchical model still relies on a **strictly matched n = 20 subset**, patch representation is **uneven**, dimensional reduction is **severe**, and **metabolomic PC1 explains only 10.0% of variance**. These limitations mean that the model can support, at most, an **exploratory summary of dominant-axis covariation**, not a strong mechanistic or highly generalizable cross-level account. Reviewer 1 is therefore justified in maintaining caution. However, neither Reviewer 1 nor the Devil’s Advocate identifies a clearly non-repairable methodological defect; rather, they identify a need for firmer positioning, clearer justification, and more explicit disclosure of analytical consequences.

The domain review is comparatively supportive and indicates that the ecological interpretation is now broadly sound so long as “colonization-year patch” is treated as an operational landscape category rather than a pure temporal state. The EIC perspective similarly suggests that this version is now plausibly publishable in a suitable invasion ecology or observational integrative venue.

Accordingly, the manuscript does **not** warrant acceptance yet, because the validated DA-critical issues have not been fully neutralized. But the manuscript also does **not** warrant Major Revision at this stage, because the central prior problems have been substantially addressed and the remaining tasks are now **bounded, clarificatory, and frame-corrective** rather than structurally reconstructive.

### Revision Checklist

#### Priority 1 — Structural Revisions

- [ ]  Reframe the hierarchical model explicitly as exploratory/integrative.
- [ ]  State prominently that the model rests on 20 strictly matched samples with uneven patch representation.
- [ ]  Reposition “colonization-year patch” as an operational classification rather than a clean temporal state.
- [ ]  Reduce any residual implication that the integrative model resolves mechanism.

#### Priority 2 — Content Supplementation

- [ ]  Justify PC1-only dimensional reduction and discuss its limitations directly.
- [ ]  Add concise convergence/ESS reporting for major model parameters.
- [ ]  Clarify the purpose and identifiability rationale for sample-level random effects.
- [ ]  State alternative reasons for weak omics–phenotype links beyond scale dependence.
- [ ]  Clarify PCA inputs and SNP-set definition for the integrative model.

#### Priority 3 — Text and Formatting

- [ ]  Soften any remaining strong causal or confirmatory wording.
- [ ]  Keep all model-fit language clearly descriptive.
- [ ]  Briefly note possible influence of transcriptome QC variation if matched samples are affected.
