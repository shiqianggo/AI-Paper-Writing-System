Based on a thorough review of the provided manuscript and supplementary materials, here is the internal consistency assessment.

### 1. Overall consistency assessment

The manuscript presents a compelling, novel, and well-structured storyline mapping the multi-omics hierarchy of _Spartina alterniflora_ during a microgeographic invasion. The logical progression from spatial-temporal variations to rapid genetic sorting, driven through intermediate omics, and culminating in the concept of "phenotypic buffering," handles the biological hierarchies impressively.

However, the current draft feels like a "near-final but incomplete" version. There are numerous explicitly written placeholders (`[Data Required]`, `[Reference Needed]`, etc.), a glaring duplication of a whole section, and significant, substantial contradictions between the sampling design described in the Methods versus the data presented in the Results and Supporting Information. These mechanical and factual errors must be cleaned up before submission.

### 2. Terminology inconsistencies

- **Microgeographic Zones:** Section 3.1 and Figure 1 explicitly introduce a secondary classification of three microgeographic zones: _"the estuary zone... competition zone... and dense zone"_. However, these specific zones are entirely absent from the Methods (Section 2.1), which merely introduces the four colonization-year patches. They must be accurately defined in the Methods first.
- **Plots vs. Quadrats:** The manuscript switches between "quadrats" (Methods 2.1) and "plots" (Tables S1 & S2). It is best to standardize the nomenclature to a single term across the main text and supplementary files.

### 3. Logic / storyline inconsistencies

- **Major Sampling Discrepancies:** This is the most critical issue in the manuscript.
    - **Main text claim:** Section 2.1 states: _"a total of 20 sampling sites were established... with five randomly placed quadrats... in each patch."_ (4 patches × 5 = 20 total)
    - **Table S1 & S2 reality:** The phenotypic data show 26 plots unevenly distributed: 6 for 2012, 11 for 2013, 4 for 2014, and 5 for 2015.
    - **Table S3 reality:** Text S1 notes that for a sample named `2012_14_8`, the last number (`8`) represents the quadrat. This means transcriptome data were collected from a "quadrat 8" in 2012, even though Tables S1 and S2 only go up to plot 6 for the year 2012.
    - **Action required:** The authors must rewrite the sampling description in Section 2.1 to reflect _exactly_ what was done in the field rather than depicting a perfectly balanced experimental design that is clearly contradicted by the raw data.
- **Bayesian Model Sample Size:** Section 2.4 mentions that 20 perfectly paired samples were retained for the Bayesian model (after metabolomic batch issue removals). Given the very skewed plot totals just identified above, it is vital to briefly state how these 20 samples were distributed across the four years (e.g., exactly 5 per year?) to reassure the reader that the model isn't heavily biased toward 2013 (which had 11 plots) versus 2014 (which had 4 plots).

### 4. Conclusion-strength inconsistencies

The consistency of conclusion strength is excellent. The authors do a great job maintaining a realistic view of their statistics. The manuscript clearly frames the omics-phenotype relations as showing "mixed or weakly constrained pathway coefficients" (Results), which smoothly and appropriately bridges to the concept of "decoupling" (Abstract/Discussion) and "phenotypic buffering." You correctly avoid overstating the causal mathematical links where P-values dictate a weak structural association.

- **Duplicated Section:** Section 2.5 ( _2.5 | Statistical analyses_ ) is duplicated word-for-word. The exact same paragraph is pasted twice back-to-back.
